Malaga's Rich History and Thriving Industrial Landscape in Perth

Posted by on


Malaga, located in the City of Swan, Perth, has an interesting etymological background, with its name thought to derive either from the Spanish city of Málaga or the Aboriginal word "malaga," meaning ironstone. This name was officially adopted in 1969, marking a significant step in the suburb's development.

Today, Malaga is known for its industrial significance, housing over 2,400 businesses and employing more than 12,000 people, as reported by the latest census. Despite its busy commercial nature, the suburb still has roots in its humble beginnings, with the 2006 census reporting only 28 residents at the time.

Malaga is well-equipped with essential facilities, including a Transperth bus depot operated by Path Transit, which supports the suburb’s transport infrastructure. It is served by several bus routes, such as the 344, 360, 361, 362, 378, and 449, providing access to various key locations like Morley Bus Station, Warwick Station, and Perth Busport. These services offer convenient connections for both the local workforce and residents, ensuring easy access to surrounding areas.

As Malaga continues to grow, its mix of industrial importance, historical context, and robust transportation network makes it a key area in Perth’s urban landscape.
